Five Tips for May

01
Get morning light early

10–15 minutes of natural light within an hour of waking anchors your circadian rhythm, improves mood, and supports better sleep at night.

02
Walk after meals

A short post-meal walk — even 10 minutes — reduces blood sugar spikes and supports digestion. Simple, consistent, and effective.

03
Name what you feel

Research shows that labeling emotions — "I feel anxious" rather than "I feel bad" — meaningfully reduces their intensity.

04
Hydrate more in warm weather

Warmer May temperatures increase your fluid needs. Aim for at least half your body weight in ounces of water daily.

05
Reduce morning allergy exposure

Pollen is highest between 5–10am. Rinse hair before bed, shower after time outdoors, and keep windows closed in the early morning.

May 2026 Spotlight

Mental Health Awareness Month

We treat anxiety, depression, and insomnia as part of our standard primary care scope. Here's what we think every patient should know this May.

💬
You Don't Need a Crisis to Come In
Mental health care works best as ongoing care, not reactive care. If stress, low mood, or anxiety has been affecting your daily life for a couple of weeks, that's reason enough to schedule a visit.
🧘
Anxiety, Depression & Insomnia Are Treated Here
Our providers offer care for anxiety, depression, and sleep disorders — including medication management and referrals to behavioral health specialists when appropriate.
🤝
Stress Management Is a Medical Issue
Chronic stress affects cardiovascular health, immune function, blood sugar, and sleep. We address it as part of a comprehensive care plan — not a side conversation.
😴
Sleep and Mental Health Are Deeply Connected
Poor sleep is both a symptom and a driver of mental health challenges. If you're not sleeping well, talk to your provider — it's one of the most impactful things we can address together.
🌿
Hormones Can Affect Mood Too
Hormone imbalances — especially during perimenopause or menopause — can significantly affect mood, energy, and cognition. Our bio-identical hormone therapy program may be worth discussing.
📋
When to Reach Out for Help
For a crisis, call or text 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline). For everything else — burnout, mood changes, ongoing stress — we're here. Call us at (423) 867-4969.
